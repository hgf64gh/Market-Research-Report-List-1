<p><p>An Aircraft Auxiliary Power Unit (APU) is a compact, onboard engine that provides power to aircraft systems when the main engines are not running. It is primarily used to supply electrical power, start the main engines, and provide air conditioning or pneumatic power while the aircraft is on the ground. APUs are critical for reducing dependency on ground power units and enhancing operational efficiency.</p><p>The Aircraft Auxiliary Power Unit (APU) Market is experiencing steady growth, driven by increasing air travel demand, advancements in aviation technology, and the need for fuel-efficient and lightweight APUs. The market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.4% during the forecast period. Key factors contributing to this growth include the rising production of commercial and military aircraft, the modernization of existing fleets, and the growing emphasis on reducing carbon emissions.</p><p>Recent trends in the APU market include the development of hybrid-electric APUs, integration of advanced materials for weight reduction, and the adoption of digital monitoring systems for predictive maintenance. Additionally, manufacturers are focusing on enhancing APU efficiency and reliability to meet stringent environmental regulations. The Asia-Pacific region is emerging as a significant market due to increasing air traffic and expanding airline fleets, further propelling the growth of the APU market globally.</p></p>
