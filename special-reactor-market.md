<p><p>Special reactor markets are categorized into non-shielded and shielded reactors based on their radiation protection features. Non-shielded reactors are designed without additional protective barriers, making them more compact and cost-effective for applications with lower radiation concerns. In contrast, shielded reactors incorporate robust containment structures to mitigate radiation exposure, ensuring safety in environments where personnel or the public may be present. Each type serves distinct operational needs, balancing safety requirements and operational efficiency in various industries, including energy and research.</p></p>
