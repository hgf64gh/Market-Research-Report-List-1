<p><p>Hydro turbine generator units are classified into several types based on design and operational principles. Francis turbines, the most common, are used for moderate head applications and achieve high efficiency. Kaplan turbines are adjustable blade devices suited for low head scenarios, facilitating energy generation in diverse water conditions. Pelton turbines, ideal for high head and low flow, utilize impulse force from water jets. Other types include turbine systems tailored for specific needs, enhancing flexibility in hydroelectric power generation.</p></p>
