<p><p>Controlled-release drug delivery technology encompasses various methods aimed at achieving targeted and sustained medication release. The Wurster technique involves spray drying for encapsulating drugs in polymers. Coacervation encapsulates therapeutic agents in a polymer shell through liquid-liquid phase separation. Microencapsulation involves encasing drugs in microscopic particles for gradual release. Implants provide localized medication release over extended periods, while transdermal systems deliver drugs through the skin. Targeted delivery focuses on directing drugs to specific sites, enhancing efficacy while minimizing side effects. Other methods may include nanoparticle systems and biodegradable systems.</p></p>
