<p><p>The Huntington's Disease treatment market is primarily divided into two categories: symptomatic therapy and disease-modifying therapy. Symptomatic therapy focuses on alleviating the specific symptoms associated with the disease, such as chorea, depression, and cognitive decline, often using medications to enhance quality of life. Disease-modifying therapy aims to address the underlying biological mechanisms of Huntington's, potentially slowing disease progression or altering its course. This distinction highlights the varying approaches to managing this complex neurodegenerative condition.</p></p>
