<p><p>The ternary materials market, primarily focused on battery applications, encompasses two key types: cathode materials and negative materials. Cathode materials, typically composed of lithium, nickel, manganese, and cobalt, are crucial for energy density and performance in lithium-ion batteries, enhancing energy storage capabilities. Negative materials, commonly made of graphite or silicon, serve as the anode in these batteries, facilitating lithium ion absorption and discharge. Together, these materials play a vital role in advancing battery technology for electric vehicles and renewable energy storage.</p></p>
