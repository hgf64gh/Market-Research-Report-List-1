<p><p>The agricultural chelates market comprises various types, including EDTA, EDDHA, DTPA, and IDHA. EDTA (Ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid) is widely used for its ability to bind essential nutrients like calcium and magnesium. EDDHA (EthyleneDiamine-N,N'-diacetic Acid) is effective for iron chelation in alkaline soils. DTPA (Diethylenetriaminepentaacetic Acid) enhances nutrient availability under varying pH levels. IDHA (Isopropyl Diamine Hydroxy Acetic Acid) targets specific metal ions, while other chelates may include synthetic or natural compounds for broader applications in agriculture.</p></p>
