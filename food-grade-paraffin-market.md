<p><p>The food-grade paraffin market comprises two primary types: solid and liquid paraffin. Solid paraffin, often used in coatings and emulsions, offers a protective layer for food products, enhancing shelf life while maintaining moisture. Liquid paraffin, on the other hand, serves as an anti-foaming agent, lubricant, or release agent in food processing. Both types are derived from refining petroleum and adhere to safety standards, making them suitable for various food applications, ensuring quality and safety in food production.</p></p>
