<p><p>Architectural films encompass various plastic types utilized in construction and design. LLDPE (Linear Low-Density Polyethylene) provides flexibility and durability, while LDPE (Low-Density Polyethylene) offers excellent transparency. HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene) is known for its strength. PP/BOPP (Polypropylene/ Biaxially Oriented Polypropylene) is lightweight and versatile, whereas PET/BOPET (Polyethylene Terephthalate) features high clarity and barrier properties. Polyamide (BOPA) provides flexibility and resistance, while PVB (Polyvinyl Butyral) enhances adhesion. PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride) is common for its durability, with “Others” referring to niche films tailored for specific applications.</p></p>
