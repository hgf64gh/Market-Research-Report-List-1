<p><p>The self-expandable intracranial stent market features two main types: open-loop and closed-loop stents. Open-loop stents are designed with a continuous structure that allows flexibility and effective vessel support, making them suitable for various anatomical configurations. Closed-loop stents possess interconnected segments, enhancing stability and reducing the risk of migration. Both types are used to treat conditions like intracranial aneurysms and stenosis, with specific design features catering to different clinical needs and patient anatomies in neurosurgical procedures.</p></p>
