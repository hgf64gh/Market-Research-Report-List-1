<p><p>The wood repair epoxy market is primarily divided into two types: liquid and putty versions. The liquid version is ideal for penetrating cracks and seams in wood, offering excellent adhesion and a smooth finish. It is commonly used for larger repairs and applications requiring a strong bond. Conversely, the putty version is thicker and easier to mold, making it suitable for filling gaps, holes, and surface repairs. Both types provide durable, long-lasting solutions for restoring and enhancing wooden structures.</p></p>
