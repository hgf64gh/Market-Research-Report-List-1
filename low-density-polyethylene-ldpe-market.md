<p><p>Low-Density Polyethylene (LDPE) is a versatile plastic known for its flexibility, low density, and resistance to moisture. The LDPE market is primarily segmented into two production processes: the autoclave process and the tubular process. The autoclave process involves polymerizing ethylene in a closed reactor, leading to a high degree of branching and properties suitable for film applications. In contrast, the tubular process utilizes a continuous flow system, producing LDPE with better clarity and strength, making it ideal for various packaging applications.</p></p>
