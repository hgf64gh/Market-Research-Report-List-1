<p><p>The brake cleaner market is primarily divided into two types: chlorinated and non-chlorinated. Chlorinated brake cleaners contain solvents like tetrachloroethylene, offering powerful cleaning capabilities and quick evaporation, making them effective for removing tough grease and grime. However, they can pose environmental and health hazards due to their toxicity. Non-chlorinated types utilize less harmful solvents, making them more environmentally friendly and safer for users, but may require more effort to achieve the same cleaning effectiveness as chlorinated versions.</p></p>
