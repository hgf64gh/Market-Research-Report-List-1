<p><p>The brake disc market primarily features two types: cast iron and carbon-matrix composite (CMC). Cast iron discs are widely used due to their excellent heat dissipation, durability, and affordability, making them a staple in conventional vehicles. CMC, on the other hand, is recognized for its lightweight properties and superior thermal performance, often found in high-performance and luxury vehicles. While cast iron dominates the mainstream market, CMC is gaining traction in niche segments, driven by advancements in materials technology.</p></p>
